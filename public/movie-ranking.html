Top 3 Ghibli Films

Sean's Personal Favorites

Spirited Away

Overview:

A mainstream staple for any Ghibli fans and for a reason. It follows the story of Chihiro the protagonist having been transported to the realms of Yokai, and this is quoted as is from wikipedias's description, Yōkai (妖怪; Japanese pronunciation: [joː.kai][1]) are a class of supernatural entities and spirits in Japanese folklore. We follow Chihiro's journey in navigating this otherworldy place, surviving dangerous encounters, romance, maturity and resolve.

Howl's Moving Castle

Overview:

Another beloved Ghibli classic, Howl's Moving Castle follows the journey of Sophie, a bright, determined, and remarkably charming young woman who is cursed by a spiteful witch after a chance encounter at her hat shop. Seeking a way to break the curse, Sophie crosses paths with the enigmatic and captivating wizard Howl. Together with Howl, his apprentice Markl, and the fiery demon Calcifer, she embarks on an adventure within Howl’s magical moving castle—an enchanting, mechanical wonder that any steampunk enthusiast would dream of calling home.

Their journey is anything but simple: each of them carries personal burdens and inner struggles, all while navigating a war-torn world where destructive modern weaponry collides with dark, malevolent magic.

Princess Mononoke

Overview:

An absolute must watch masterpiece by Hayao Miyazaki. We watch as the protagonist, Ashitaka, saves his village from a Demon, a fallen god, only to end up having his right arm cursed by the demon's corruption. He is then vanished, a decision he wholeheartedly accepts. He then sets out on a journey to discover what had led to the Demon's appearance at their village. This then leads him to meeting the old gods, the warring factions and Princess Mononoke who is an enigma, having been adopted by a god as her daughter and now lives among them inside the forest where the gods reside.
